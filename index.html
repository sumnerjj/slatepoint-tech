The compliance cost is climbing.

Under the 2022 Construction General Permit, California's State Water Resources Control Board tightened numeric effluent limits, inspection cadence, and reporting requirements. A Caltrans analysis estimated annual compliance costs could nearly double — from ~$175M to $325-348M statewide.

And the top three violation categories in SMARTS haven't changed in a decade:

  • Late reports

    The #1 violation category statewide — 1,800+ formal notices in a single fiscal year.

  • Deficient BMP implementation

    Control measures not maintained, inspected, or documented on the required cadence.

  • Incomplete or missing SWPPPs

    Plan updates missed after site changes; version drift between the office binder and the site.

None of these are failures of skill. They're failures of process — the paperwork gets ahead of the spreadsheet, and a missed checkbox turns into a violation weeks later.
